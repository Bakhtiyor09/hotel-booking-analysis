## Results
- Bookings and revenue show strong seasonality, with peak performance during mid-year months and weaker demand in off-season periods.

- City Hotels generate higher total revenue than Resort Hotels, indicating stronger and more consistent year-round demand.

- Cancellation volumes increase during peak demand periods, suggesting the influence of flexible cancellation policies and demand uncertainty.

- Cancellation rates rise significantly with longer lead times, showing that bookings made far in advance carry higher cancellation risk.

- Revenue trends closely follow booking volumes, confirming that overall performance is primarily demand-driven rather than price-driven.
